Topology and Geometry in Physics

Information

Lecturer

Ling-Wei Luo

Course Description

This course examines the interplay between topology, geometry, and physics. It begins with the study of curves and surfaces in classical differential geometry. The course then introduces differential forms, followed by Cartan’s methods of moving frames and exterior calculus. Building on these foundations, we develop the theory of differential manifolds and fiber bundles from a modern geometric perspective. The course concludes with selected topics in algebraic topology and their applications in physics.
